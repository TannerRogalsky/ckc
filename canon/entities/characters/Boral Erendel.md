A member of [[The Order of Seasons]] stationed at [[The Garden]]. Advised that to understand empire and imperial structure, one should "look east if you want your answers."

[[Veronia Karadel]] worries about his whereabouts and side project at the Garden, asking the party to convince him to return to [[House Kiernodel]] so she can have a word with him.

In session 015, [[Red Caesar]] and [[Ceril]] visited Boral at his side office at the Garden. He revealed he left the Order because they had him "locked up in a side room" doing spellcraft without a view, "as though we were still slaves." He has been working on his own version of the [[Demi-Spell]] to make it "better" than [[Keys]] would allow. While Keys wants chaos to reign to some extent, Boral wants order to prevail. His plan is eugenics: he has already created thousands of empty high elf forms, and when the Demi-Spell is cast, all spirits will be incarnated into these perfect high elf bodies. He believes this would eliminate war, which "arises from differences" and "fears of the other." He told Red, "better, yes, and better than myself, even" — implying humans like Red would not be preserved in their current form. He said if Keys doesn't allow him to integrate his ideas, he may forge ahead on his own.

Red and Ceril were horrified by his plan. Ceril called him an apostate whose inherent dangers magnify the Demi-Spell's evil "ten times." He proposed capturing or assassinating the [[Rakshasa]] as leverage against Keys to halt the Demi-Spell entirely.

In session 022, [[Red Caesar]] reported to [[Risolvir Kirnodell]] that Boral had gone "off the rails" at [[The Garden]], adding nearly a hundred pages of his own design to the [[Demi-Spell]] and developing extreme political ideas. Risolvir agreed to send someone to retrieve him.
