A masked human seneschal of the [[Broyish Empire]] who serves at the Dawn Palace. Accompanied by an entourage of mostly masked humans, he intercepts visitors, takes their names, distinguishes citizens from outsiders, and controls access to the imperial high court. He read [[Red Caesar]]'s thoughts while screening the party, then granted them audience with [[Vizier Jade]] and [[Emperor Shen]]. He acts as Vizier Jade's entourage and gatekeeper, though Vizier Jade's mind-reading is presented as more powerful than his own.

In session 024, [[Domyx]] encountered Fharan near the Dawn Palace in the [[Broyish Capital]]. Fharan framed the Empire as a refuge for people who "fall through the cracks" in other societies: humans displaced by competition with elves and dwarves after the waters rose, followed by titan folk and others who swear allegiance to the rising Empire. He acknowledged that humans retain a higher social tier than later arrivals. His pitch of belonging, free movement, and advancement through work presents imperial inclusion as conditional on allegiance and effort.

Fharan immediately recognized Domyx as a member of [[Clan Akathia]] and described the clan as the noble ruling house of Nornhof and the Titan region. He claimed that the clan's supposedly restricted rites and truths are widely known beyond the mountains, particularly among those able to leave them. His account revealed to Domyx that many titan folk have migrated to the capital and challenged Domyx's sheltered understanding of his people. The extent and source of Fharan's knowledge of Akathian secrets remain unclear.

In session 031, Fharan led the imperial force that forced [[Theo Harvey]] into [[The Carrot Cake]] and reached [[Jack Harvey]]'s inner sanctum door. He recognized [[Red Caesar]] during the ensuing fight. The party defeated him and Red Caesar dispelled a lethal contingency that would have killed him before capture. While dying under [[Ceril]] and [[Kerben]]'s care, Fharan revealed that [[Obould]] was alive and being brought by [[Vizier Jade]] and [[Emperor Shen]], that they would arrive before noon, and that [[Starfall]]'s next target was the sun. He could not explain Vizier Jade's mind-reading beyond implying that her power exceeds ordinary magic. Fharan died after the interrogation.
