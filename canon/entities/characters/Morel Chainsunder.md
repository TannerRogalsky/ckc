Leader of the Duergar in [[The Deepworlders Delve]] at [[The Pit]]. The Duergar are obsessed with the [[Penumbra]] and are culty and insular. [[Figma Brickfinger's Union]] has an uneasy truce with them, though there was war in the past.

Also operates within the [[Darvinblast]] as a cult leader, exerting magical control over the dwarf population and brainwashing them into hostility toward the party. Broadcasts commands through the walls of the underground city via a booming voice system, rallying dwarf forces. His magical control was dispelled from the [[Steelfend Clan]] by [[Red Caesar]]'s level 4 Dispel Magic. The party was directed by divine consultation to find him before seeking the [[Penumbra]]. His voice broadcasts reveal his grand ambitions — during a short rest in [[Darvinblast]], he proclaimed: "Do not lose faith, my warriors. They cannot get us all. We must hold strong, because the time of redemption is at hand. We will claim the upper world if you fight off these last few. Mobilize and defend us." This reveals he plans not just to control the dwarves but to lead them to the surface world.

## The Revelation

In the church sanctuary, the party confronted what appeared to be Morel Chainsunder. He stood on a platform in smooth tar, surrounded by a congregation in red robes and caged civilians. He claimed that [[Figma Brickfinger]] had "cast them here" and that they were "forced from the light." He cast Flamestrike (geothermal fire from below, not celestial) for devastating damage against the party.

[[Ceril]] cast a divination and discovered the truth: **"Morel" is not real.** As [[Ceril]] stated, "to locate Morel is to locate nothing." The "Morel" the party fought was a composite manifestation of the dwarves' collective fears and paranoia — an illusion made real by their belief in it, similar to how the Kuatoans made [[Blibdoolpoolp]] real through belief. The face of the defeated "Morel" was different from the one seen through the illusion earlier, and when defeated he simply blended back into the congregation as "just another random member."

The real Morel Chainsunder had been dead for approximately 50 years. His skeleton was found in a hidden shrine/office, draped in cloaks before a document that was his last will and testament. The document itself was the spell — a binding magic that united the dwarves emotionally ("what one brother says or does, the others will feel"). Morel's intention was benevolent: he hoped that over decades, this shared empathy would bring them peace and lead them back to the surface. Instead, it twisted into a curse through a feedback loop — worsening opinions and anger amplified until only hatred remained. The spell also made them aware of each other's locations at all times and created the illusion of a "big brother" watching them.

The *Mirage Arcana* illusion covering the northern mountains was linked to this curse. When [[Red Caesar]] destroyed the document, the magic departed and the illusion cleared. The fear subsided from the dwarves, replaced by curiosity.

## Consultation with Aeris

[[Ceril]] contacted [[Aeris]] (Eris, goddess of paths and randomness) through his shield. Five yes/no questions were asked:
1. Is there a path to save lives AND find the Penumbra? **"Your words freed them, as the spell once freed them."**
2. Should they destroy the document? **"Destroy the spell. Old magic binds, new magic frees."**
3. Where is the Penumbra? **"In the hills, not too far away. Others might be after it."**
4. Does [[The Order of Seasons]] know how to use the Penumbra? **"Other organizations know very little of how to use it."**
5. How many Penumbra pieces exist? **"More than you believe. Many sank in the ocean, many on land."**

After the spell was broken, the party followed Ceril to where the Mirage Arcana had been hiding a quarry. There they found a massive chunk of [[Penumbra]] — 50-60 feet across, tetrahedron-shaped. The illusion may have been the only thing preventing Imperial scouts from discovering it.
