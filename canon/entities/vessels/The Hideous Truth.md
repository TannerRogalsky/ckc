A small galley captured by the party from hobgoblin pirates during a boarding attack on [[The Opal]]. Roughly a third the size of [[The Opal]], it had no papers, no colors, and no black flag — a ghost ship that attacked without warning. Its original name, "Eschaton," means "the end times" in Fae.

The ship was captained by a [[Warg Lord]] who lurked below decks. After the party defeated the Warg Lord and surviving hobgoblins, [[Obould]] proposed keeping the vessel and adding it to the fleet. With three ships now under his command (the Opal, the Gheister, and The Hideous Truth), Obould effectively became Commodore. The party recovered gold from crates that had gone overboard during the Warg Lord's howl.

The party decided they lacked the resources to crew a third ship and instead hid the vessel in a jungled grotto — a moon-shaped cape with canopy extending over the ocean, protecting it from aerial reconnaissance by the [[Broyish Empire]]. They renamed it the "Hideous Truth" and covered it with foliage for later retrieval.

In session 027, the party swung by the hidden grotto on their way to [[The Carrot Cake]] and confirmed the vessel was still hidden in the bay. Overgrowth and vines had climbed further onto the boat, making it even more concealed but also requiring cleanup before relaunching. The party chose to leave it overgrown for additional camouflage.
