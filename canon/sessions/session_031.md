## Session 031

### Chunk 0000

[[Kerben]] took command of [[The Opal]] during a naval attack by the [[Broyish Empire]]. [[Ulrich Fjoller]], [[Brim the Bullywog]], [[Yalet Mora]], and the rest of the crew helped operate the ship's upgraded weapons while imperial boats closed in. Kerben prioritized survival over trying to defeat the whole fleet, using the ship's new armaments to cripple the most dangerous escort vessel and buying room for retreat.

When Kerben spotted many more imperial sails approaching from the east, he committed to escape. He sent the bound air elemental against the damaged enemy ships and used a ghost-ship feather token to turn The Opal invisible. The maneuver confused the imperial attackers long enough for the crew to slip west, away from the battle, toward the waters near [[Lyngbakr Lagoon]].

Kerben returned alone to the entrance of [[The Carrot Cake]] the next morning. A new imperial landing party arrived with [[Theo Harvey]] in manacles, forcing him to guide them into the park. [[Tango]] scouted overhead and identified the imperial flagship offshore as the [[Imperial Xihe]]. Kerben followed the imperials inside and found they had killed the helpful [[Magen]] near the midway entrance.

The imperial group reached the sealed door to [[Jack Harvey]]'s inner sanctum but could not open it. [[Red Caesar]], [[Ceril]], [[Domyx]], and [[Vokenar]] returned through the disabled portal network by Red Caesar's magic, arriving amid a chaotic fight between the imperial force and vampire spawn from the park.

### Chunk 0001

The party fought [[Fharan]], imperial scions, imperial warlords, and vampire spawn near the inner sanctum door. Kerben used Knock to free [[Theo Harvey]] from his manacles, handed him a balloon feather token, and let him escape. Theo told Kerben that the [[Penumbra]] was behind the door, that the party could open it, and that he would hide in the dwarven lands.

Red Caesar determined that the door recognized only those who had completed the lamp challenges. He also realized the sealed chamber was holding back the post-Cataclysm ocean and would release a dangerous rush of water when opened. The party defeated the imperial force and subdued Fharan alive after Red Caesar dispelled a lethal contingency meant to prevent his capture.

With [[Ceril]] and [[Kerben]] keeping [[Fharan]] alive long enough to speak, the party questioned him. Fharan confirmed [[Obould]] was alive and being brought to the site by [[Vizier Jade]] and [[Emperor Shen]], warned that they would arrive before noon, and revealed that [[Starfall]]'s next target was [[Sigil]], the sun. He could not explain [[Vizier Jade]]'s mind-reading except to imply that her power was innate or beyond ordinary spellcasting. Fharan died after the questioning, and the party disposed of his body through the [[Hole on Wheels]] portal.

### Chunk 0002

The party prepared for the inner sanctum's pressure hazard. [[Vokenar]] shaped a stone barricade while [[Ceril]] shaped water, allowing Domyx to open the door by placing his hand against it. [[Jack Harvey]]'s magical voice welcomed them to the vault, and a surge of water flooded out into the midway.

Inside, the party entered an underwater vault and used Water Breathing to continue. [[Red Caesar]] collected vast amounts of [[Penumbra]] crystal from the walls, condensing it into a small dense orb with [[Obvolvo Caelum]]. [[Kerben]] and [[Domyx]] also found [[Jade's Compass]], a tarnished but valuable compass bearing [[Vizier Jade]]'s name, implying one of [[Vizier Jade]]'s agents had reached the vault years earlier.

Deeper in the vault, the party confronted [[The Tyrant]], revealed to be [[Vizier Jade]]'s enormous undead beholder-like servant. The Tyrant fought amid the Penumbra crystals and spoke telepathically in confusion and despair, believing the party had damaged his divine "mothers" and asking how anyone had reached [[Starfall]].

### Chunk 0003

The party wore down The Tyrant's defenses and [[Red Caesar]] used Charm Monster to end the fight without destroying him outright. While charmed, The Tyrant explained that [[Starfall]] lies beneath the world at its center, in [[Axis Mundi]]. He said the weapon was born with the world, made from [[Stark]]'s four elements, and that he himself was created by its energy.

Before The Tyrant could say more, a force tied to [[Vizier Jade]] crushed and condensed him into a bony singularity. The party recovered the immense Penumbra hoard, understood it as more than all their previous Penumbra finds combined, and used [[Vokenar]]'s Word of Recall to return to [[The Opal]]. The ship remained invisible, damaged but afloat, with no crew lost. Vokenar healed injured crew members while the crew repaired what they could.

[[The Opal]] reached [[Lyngbakr Lagoon]], where [[Alamar]]'s settlement had grown into a refuge with people from human, dwarven, and elven communities. Red Caesar explained that the [[Broyish Empire]], especially [[Emperor Shen]] and [[Vizier Jade]], had caused the sky crisis with [[Starfall]]. Alamar reported that the settlement had expanded after refugees fled the moon's destruction, forcing him to abandon secrecy and arm the island with harpoons against imperial balloons.

[[Tango]] warned Kerben that the [[Imperial Xihe]] had reached the shore and that imperial troops were landing. Red Caesar and Ceril took the condensed Penumbra to find [[Lyngbakr]] while Kerben, Domyx, Vokenar, and Alamar moved to delay the invasion.

### Chunk 0004

On the shore, [[Emperor Shen]] and [[Vizier Jade]] arrived with [[Obould]] bound, gagged, and caged. Emperor Shen demanded the Penumbra, threatened to destroy the lagoon settlement, and said [[Starfall]] would fire again after the party watched everything they loved be taken away. [[Kerben]] opened fire, wounding Emperor Shen and revealing that the emperor's decrepit appearance was partly a ruse: beneath the mask, he looked much like [[Domyx]]. Kerben then shot open Obould's restraints.

[[Domyx]] used cloud jaunt to exchange places with Obould, freeing the captain and trapping himself in the cage. Obould ordered Kerben to get him back to [[The Opal]], warning that larger dangers were at hand. Emperor Shen carried the caged Domyx away in a column of golden light, declaring that his grandson would be returned to the Empire and disciplined.

[[Vokenar]] stayed behind to delay Vizier Jade. He resisted her first attempt to banish him back to [[Arcadia]], then challenged her reasons for trying to kill the gods. Vizier Jade admitted that she had once fought for the world but had grown tired of watching wars and divine conflicts destroy lives. She killed Vokenar with a word of death and departed with his body.

Meanwhile, Red Caesar and Ceril found [[Lyngbakr]] hidden deep beneath the largest waterfall. Red Caesar released the condensed Penumbra, filling the water with star-like motes that Lyngbakr consumed. Lyngbakr declared he now held enough of the sky to finish the repairs. Ceril and Alamar went with Lyngbakr as the giant turtle rose into the heavens, shedding black sky-matter that sealed the holes across the world. The sky closed beneath them, leaving Red Caesar on Stark looking up at a whole blue sky for the first time in his life. [[The Opal]] recovered Red Caesar afterward. Obould and Kerben were safe aboard, but Domyx had been captured and Vokenar's corpse was in [[Vizier Jade]]'s possession.

### Summary

Key events:
- [[Kerben]] saved [[The Opal]] from the initial imperial naval attack by crippling enemy ships, releasing the ship's air elemental, and turning The Opal invisible.
- [[Theo Harvey]] was recaptured by the Empire, forced to guide soldiers into [[The Carrot Cake]], then freed again by Kerben with a balloon feather token.
- The party defeated [[Fharan]]'s imperial force at [[Jack Harvey]]'s inner sanctum door and learned that [[Starfall]]'s next target is [[Sigil]], the sun.
- The party opened Jack Harvey's underwater vault and recovered the largest known store of [[Penumbra]] using [[Obvolvo Caelum]].
- [[The Tyrant]] was found inside the vault, revealed as [[Vizier Jade]]'s beholder-like servant, and revealed that [[Starfall]] is located in [[Axis Mundi]].
- The party returned to The Opal and then to [[Lyngbakr Lagoon]], where the [[Broyish Empire]] launched a direct assault.
- [[Obould]] was rescued when Kerben shot his restraints and [[Domyx]] swapped places with him.
- [[Domyx]] was captured by [[Emperor Shen]], and [[Vokenar]] was killed by [[Vizier Jade]], who took his body.
- [[Lyngbakr]] consumed Jack Harvey's Penumbra and repaired the [[Hole in the Sky]], carrying [[Ceril]] and [[Alamar]] into the heavens as the sky sealed.

Items and discoveries:
- [[Jade's Compass]] was found in Jack Harvey's vault, suggesting one of [[Vizier Jade]]'s agents had reached or died inside the vault years earlier.
- The condensed Carrot Cake Penumbra represented more sky-material than all previous Penumbra finds combined.
- [[Axis Mundi]] is the location beneath the world where Starfall is hidden.
- [[Starfall]]'s next target is [[Sigil]], the sun.

Session 031 completes the Carrot Cake arc and resolves the immediate sky-repair goal. [[Jack Harvey]]'s hidden vault contained exactly the kind of concentrated Penumbra the party needed, and Red Caesar's newly completed [[Obvolvo Caelum]] made transporting it possible. By delivering that Penumbra to [[Lyngbakr]], the party finally repaired the sky itself and ended the open wound between Stark and [[Arcadia]].

The victory sharply changes the campaign's strategic problem. The sky is repaired, but [[Starfall]] still exists, [[Aeris]] remains imprisoned inside it, and the [[Broyish Empire]] still intends to fire the weapon again — this time at [[Sigil]], the sun. The party now has the first concrete location for the weapon: [[Axis Mundi]], beneath the world at Stark's center.

The session also ends with the party split and wounded at a campaign-defining cliffhanger. [[Obould]] is alive and back near [[The Opal]], but [[Domyx]] was taken by [[Emperor Shen]], [[Vokenar]] was killed by [[Vizier Jade]], and [[Ceril]] departed into the heavens with [[Lyngbakr]] and [[Alamar]]. Red Caesar remains on Stark with the repaired sky above him and the next objective below: reach [[Axis Mundi]] before the Empire fires [[Starfall]] again.

### Connections

- The Carrot Cake lead from [[Theo Harvey]] and [[Kerben]]'s bargain in session 024 is vindicated: [[Jack Harvey]] truly hid enough Penumbra there to repair the sky.
- Red Caesar's [[Obvolvo Caelum]] breakthrough from session 030 immediately becomes essential, allowing the party to condense the entire vault's Penumbra into a portable form.
- [[The Tyrant]]'s fate clarifies the long-running question from sessions 023–024: [[Vizier Jade]]'s lost servant reached the Carrot Cake vault and became trapped or abandoned there.
- The [[Axis Mundi]] revelation advances the Starfall quest from conjecture to a concrete destination.
- [[Fharan]]'s warning that [[Starfall]]'s next target is [[Sigil]] escalates [[Emperor Shen]]'s war on the sister goddesses after the attacks on [[Aeris]] and [[Crone]].
- [[Obould]]'s rescue resolves the hostage leverage [[Vizier Jade]] had held over [[Lady Jacinthe]] and [[The League of New Stark]] since session 021.
- [[Lyngbakr]] repairing the sky fulfills [[Crone]]'s command to focus on restoring [[Aeris]] rather than the moon.
- [[Alamar]] leaving Stark with Lyngbakr changes the future of [[Lyngbakr Lagoon]], which had grown around his leadership and protection.
- [[Vokenar]] resisting banishment shows his commitment to Stark despite his origin in [[Arcadia]], but [[Vizier Jade]]'s killing blow leaves his fate dependent on recovering his body or divine intervention.
- [[Domyx]]'s capture by [[Emperor Shen]] brings the [[Clan Akathia]] bloodline conflict directly back into the Empire's hands.
