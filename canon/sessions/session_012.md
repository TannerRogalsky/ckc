# Session 012

## Events

- **Pre-session chat:** Discussion about future campaigns including cyberpunk settings and Gravity Jackers. [[Ceril]] will remain in the dungeon and be DM-controlled. Party's "Hatred Meter" with the dwarves is at level 2 (Wrath), down from higher. Lust meter is at 71/85 (improving).
- **Preparations at Steelfend HQ:** [[Kerben]] cast Stone Cunning to map ahead. [[Red Caesar]] cast Ritual Detect Magic. [[Vokunar]] cast Water Walk ritual. The gunky ash/soot areas count as liquid for spell purposes.
- **Exploration deeper into Darvinblast:** The city features narrow tunnels resembling city streets with an "ant farm" feel. The ground level is dominated by a **fetid black mist** that crawls along the floor — a poison hazard (DC 16 con save, 19 poison damage on fail, with max HP drain equal to damage taken). Residents live elevated in wall-dwellings accessed by wooden ladders. [[Vokunar]] and [[Red Caesar]] failed their saves and lost max HP. [[Kerben]] and [[Domyx]] succeeded (Kerben took only 4 damage with poison resistance).
- **Combat — Etten and Deep World Wizard:** As the party rounded a corner, a champion emerged from the walls: an [[Etten]] (two-headed giant) wielding a battle axe and morning star, accompanied by a [[Deep World Wizard]]. The wizard was fumbling with his spellbook as he leaped into combat.
- **Combat round 1:** [[Ceril]] wild shaped, fired Luminous Arrow, then cast Fog Cloud. [[Red Caesar]] cast Magic Missile — the wizard attempted to counterspell (level 3) but Red's con save (16) beat it, and the missiles connected for 10 damage. The wizard then cast Fireball (level 4) for 30 fire damage on Red and Ceril (both failed dex saves). Red survived due to Ceril's Warding Flare. The Etten engaged [[Domyx]], knocking him prone and dealing 11 damage with its axe and morning star.
- **Combat round 2:** [[Kerben]] used Hunter's Mark and shot the Etten with his musket. [[Vokunar]] cast Toll the Dead for 11 damage (Etten failed wis save). [[Domyx]] stood up and attacked with his halberd (Darkenhoff), bringing the Etten to near death. The wizard attempted a force grab on Domyx for 24 force damage (reduced to 20 with temp HP from Vokunar's Warding Flare). Domyx used Sentinel to attack the wizard before he could grab Red, knocking him off balance. The wizard missed Red twice and used Misty Step to retreat into melee range with Kerben and Vokunar.
- **Wizard's defeat:** [[Kerben]] switched from musket to dual scimitars, dealing 33 damage with sneak attack and [[Zulu]]'s charge. [[Domyx]] chased down the retreating wizard and delivered the final blow. The wizard cast Hellish Rebuke (level 2) as a dying attack — 17 fire damage to Domyx, reduced to 8 via dex save.
- **Etten's defeat:** [[Domyx]] finished off the Etten with a two-headed chop using his halberd, then used both Nimbus bonus actions to heal.
- **Aftermath:** [[Ceril]] wild shaped from arrow mode to healer mode, dealing 24 healing to Red Caesar. [[Vokunar]] cast Aura of Vitality (level 3) to heal the party. Party surpassed 100 XP — eligible to level up on next long rest.
- **Mysterious benefactor:** A dwarf voice called down from a window above, praising Domyx's courage and questioning [[Morel Chainsunder]]'s plans ("Was it for all of us? Or just a privileged few?"). He tossed Domyx a [[Potion of Proof Against Storms]] (thunder and lightning resistance for 1 hour).
- **Looting:** [[Vokunar]] and [[Ceril]] found crates of minted Deepworlder coins. [[Kerben]] found a rare ruby embedded in a newly-mined wall section (worth 10x normal finds).

## Characters & Plot Summary

The party continued deeper into [[Darvinblast]] from the [[Steelfend Clan]] headquarters, leaving relative safety behind. The environment itself became a hazard — a toxic black mist dominates the ground level of the city, permanently damaging those who breathe it. The dwarves who live here have adapted by elevating their homes above the mist, but the poison takes a cumulative toll. Vokunar and Red Caesar both lost max HP, a mechanic that persists until a long rest.

The combat encounter introduced two new enemy types: the [[Etten]], a two-headed giant that serves as a champion fighter, and a [[Deep World Wizard]], a skilled combat mage who lives in a large dwelling within the city. The wizard proved dangerous — his Fireball nearly took out Red and Ceril, and his force attacks bypassed Domyx's rage damage reduction. However, the party's coordination was effective: Kerben's switch from ranged to melee caught the wizard off guard, and Domyx's Sentinel and relentless pursuit finished the fight.

A significant narrative moment came when a mysterious dwarf from the walls tossed Domyx a potion and questioned Morel Chainsunder's motives. This suggests there may be dissent among the dwarf population — not all are fully committed to Morel's cause, and some may see the party as potential allies rather than enemies. The dwarf's words ("Face the sun. Face the light of day again") hint at a desire for the dwarves to return to the surface.

The party is now deeper in the city, having proven their combat capability against the local defenders. They have XP sufficient to level up and have accumulated treasure (coins and a rare ruby). The search for [[Morel Chainsunder]] continues.

## Chunk 2 — Ambush and Shrine

- **Interacting with the dwarf population:** The party approached a residential zone where dwarf families lived in wall-dwellings. A child dwarf called out fearfully; [[Vokunar]] reassured them that they were not there to harm anyone and that the dwarves had the choice to leave if they wanted. The child's parents quickly pulled them back inside.
- **Second ambush:** [[Kerben]]'s tremor sense detected enemies moving through the walls, getting into position above. The party was not caught by surprise. The ambush consisted of grunts, a priest, and another [[Deep World Wizard]].
- **Combat — ambush round 1:** [[Domyx]] engaged a grunt and killed it efficiently with two attacks. [[Vokunar]] cast Sacred Flame at the priest (missed). The priest cast Radiant Flame at Vokunar (missed twice). The wizard cast Blight (level 4) on Domyx for 36 necrotic damage — described as rapid aging with dust coming off his body. [[Kerben]] used Hunter's Mark and sniped the Deep World priest for 38 damage, obliterating him in a single volley.
- **Combat — ambush round 2:** [[Red Caesar]] cast Shield of Faith (AC 17). [[Ceril]] summoned an aerial beast and flew it behind the wizard. The wizard attempted a level 3 spell but [[Red Caesar]] Counterspelled it successfully — visualized as [[Master Lee]]'s head blowing a magical wind to fizzle the casting. The wizard cast Magic Missile and Primal Savagery (claws) on the wizard.
- **Wizard's defeat:** The wizard Misty Stepped beside [[Vokunar]] to threaten [[Ceril]] — a fatal mistake given [[Kerben]]'s turn order. [[Domyx]] delivered the final blow; the wizard cast Hellish Rebuke as a dying reaction but Domyx took only 6 fire damage with advantage on his dex save.
- **Investigation — shrine:** The party discovered a shrine with ivory figurines representing three main dwarvish deities. Unlike the sister goddesses (Aeris, Sigil, Crone) who occupy the sky, these dwarf gods represent the "deepest of the deep" — the anti-sky. The figurines were of decent value and taken to the hold.
- **Looting:** [[Red Caesar]] found a jade chunk embedded in an unmined wall section (using the same technique [[Kerben]] used earlier to find the ruby).
- **Short rest:** The party took a short rest. [[Vokunar]] used Channel Divinity and Cure Wounds to heal Domyx. During the rest, [[Morel Chainsunder]]'s voice broadcast again: "Do not lose faith, my warriors... We will claim the upper world if you fight off these last few."
- **Approaching reinforcements:** The party spotted an enormous triceratops-like mount (larger than an elephant, with a beak and three horns) carrying an omnibus with five people including grunts and a spy — reinforcements sent by Morel.

## Chunk 3 — Triceratops Tamed and Fire Giant

- **Morel's broadcast:** "There they are. Prepare a final stand."
- **Triceratops encounter:** [[Kerben]] cast Speak with Animals to understand the mount's commands. [[Red Caesar]] cast Fireball (level 4) — killed the spy, torched the grunts. [[Vokunar]] cast Ice Ball (level 4) — decimated the remaining grunts. [[Domyx]] finished the commandant.
- **Taming the triceratops:** The triceratops went wild after its rider fell, charging [[Ceril]] for 27 damage. [[Vokunar]] cast Calm Emotions (level 2) — the beast failed its charisma save with a natural 0. Kerben communicated with it: it was born above ground, brought down young, and fed things it shouldn't eat. [[Domyx]] helped calm it with his presence. Kerben offered "stone bark" (surface food) which won it over. The party mounted the triceratops and rode it deeper.
- **Short rest on the triceratops:** [[Vokunar]] used Channel Divinity on Domyx (max heal). Kerben found command postings in Northern for directing the triceratops.
- **Fire Giant encounter:** Beyond a bridge over a 60-foot pit of burning miasma, a [[Fire Giant]] leapt from the wall, engulfed in flames. It spoke Giant's Runic: "Finally, a fight worth my while!" Acknowledged "gunpowder from the east."
- **Fire Giant combat:** [[Kerben]] commanded the triceratops to charge — knocked the giant down and pushed it off the bridge into the 60-foot pit. Kerben shot the falling giant with Ensnaring Strike (level 2). [[Domyx]] used Enlarge and leaped into the pit, grappled the giant with an elbow drop. Took 21+21 fire damage from the giant's flame sword. The giant recognized Domyx as "one giant to another" and told him to "return to the sky." [[Vokunar]] cast Healing Word and Toll the Dead. [[Ceril]] landed the finishing blow. Vokunar swooped down to rescue [[Zulu]] (who Kerben had jumped into the pit).
- **Aftermath:** Short rest. [[Kerben]] healed Domyx (19 HP). [[Vokunar]] used Channel Divinity (7 HP). The area was a planned expansion zone for deeper drilling.

## Chunk 4 — The Church of Morel

- **Entering the church:** [[Kerben]] used Knock (keyring charge) on the arcane-locked back door. Morel answered: "Aye, enter, will ye."
- **The sanctuary:** Morel stood on a platform in smooth bubbling tar. Congregation in red robes filled pews. Cages held civilian prisoners. Morel declared the party came to "take away our future, sent down by the goddesses of above."
- **Vokunar's persuasion:** [[Vokunar]] dismounted and addressed the congregation, arguing for unity and freedom. Morel grew worried he was losing their support.
- **Flamestrike:** Morel cast Flamestrike (geothermal fire, not celestial) — [[Domyx]] took 51 fire damage (devastated, on last HP). [[Kerben]] and others took 25. Morel claimed "[[Figma Brickfinger]] cast us here, we were forced from the light."
- **Domyx's sky-blue hands:** Near death, Domyx showed the congregation his hands — the fingertips that touched the sky remain permanently blue, matching the current sky color. Proof of the surface world. [[Vokunar]] lifted his hands and gave a speech about freedom and beauty beyond the cave.
- **Kerben shoots Morel:** [[Kerben]] Hunter'sMarked Morel and shot him. Morel's face was revealed — a dwarf with tentacle-like painted hair. The congregation was not alarmed since they saw Morel as all-powerful, but the shots were landing.
- **Freeing prisoners:** [[Red Caesar]] freed caged civilians by removing bolts.
- **Morel's counter:** Morel cast a spell (Holy Word?) at Vokunar — Vokunar used Guarded Mind (subclass feature) to resist, taking only 4 radiant damage.

## Chunk 5 — The Truth About Morel and the Penumbra

- **Ceril's divination:** [[Ceril]] cast a divination and walked to Vokunar's side: "We came here looking for Morel. This creature is not Morel. To locate Morel is to locate nothing." Morel was a composite illusion of the dwarves' collective paranoia and fear.
- **Breaking Morel's power:** [[Vokunar]] continued persuasion with advantage — Morel weakened from 7d10 psychic damage. [[Domyx]] gave a speech about self-determination, removing cowls from congregants to show them as individuals. [[Kerben]] kept shooting — Morel bled, looking very human. [[Vokunar]] noticed this face was different from the Morel seen through the illusion earlier.
- **Morel defeated:** [[Vokunar]] cast Burning Hands (radiant) — Morel passed out. He blended into the congregation as "just another random member." The real Morel was never a single person; anyone could have become "Morel" through the spell.
- **The skeleton and the spell:** [[Ceril]]'s divination led the party to a hidden shrine/office. They found a skeleton draped in cloaks — the real [[Morel Chainsunder]], dead ~50 years. Before him was his last will and testament, which was the binding spell itself. [[Red Caesar]] cast Detect Magic and Identify. The spell was meant to unite the dwarves emotionally but twisted into a curse through a feedback loop of anger and fear.
- **Consultation with Aeris:** [[Ceril]] contacted [[Aeris]] through his shield. Five yes/no questions:
  1. Path to save lives AND find Penumbra? "Your words freed them, as the spell once freed them."
  2. Destroy the document? "Destroy the spell. Old magic binds, new magic frees."
  3. Where is the Penumbra? "In the hills, not too far away. Others might be after it."
  4. Order of Seasons and Penumbra? "Other organizations know very little of how to use it."
  5. How many Penumbra pieces? "More than you believe. Many sank in the ocean, many on land."
- **Breaking the curse:** [[Red Caesar]] destroyed the document. The magic departed. Fear subsided in the city, replaced by curiosity.
- **The Penumbra revealed:** [[Kerben]] spotted a pinpoint of blue light — an escape shaft straight up. Ceril led the party to where the Mirage Arcana had been hiding a quarry. There they found a massive chunk of [[Penumbra]] — 50-60 feet across, tetrahedron-shaped. The deep worlders had been harnessing energy from it. The illusion may have been the only thing preventing Imperial scouts from finding it.
- **Looting:** Found texts on the deep worlders. Distributed treasure: 10 uncommons and rares including gems (amethysts, rubies, jade, citrine), mithril, and a holy mask of Ogun (dwarven god of darkness).
