## Chunks

### Chunk 0000

The party enters a flooded guest room in [[Castle Kaedon]]. Through a collapsed ceiling, [[Vokunar]] discovers [[Sunspite]]'s severed head — the one he chopped off in their earlier fight — crushed under rubble and overgrown with roses. [[Vokunar]] also finds [[Lady Acelia's Chalice]], an expertly carved oak chalice set with jade insets. [[Red Caesar]] identifies [[Lady Acelia]] as the younger sister of [[Alamar]] and youngest daughter of the Rodgard family, the last child born before their father [[King Maniasis]] succumbed to madness. After the crown was abdicated, Acelia reportedly traveled north to pursue peace efforts with the dwarves, and likely perished in [[The Cataclysm]].

[[Red Caesar]] uncovers bars of mithril hidden beneath floorboards — someone's attempt to hide trade goods for a potential escape to elf territory — along with gilded puzzle toys that may have belonged to [[Alamar]] himself. The party also finds a [[Potion of Eels]] (grants 21 Dexterity for one hour) and a [[Potion of Clairvoyance]] (clairvoyance for 10 minutes without concentration), both inventions of [[Lenth the Rugged]].

The party enters a presentation hall filled with silvery [[Akasha]] mist. The mist coagulates into specters and a wraith, ambushing the party. [[Vokunar]] casts Daylight to disadvantage the undead, but the spell triggers the Akasha hazard — the mist drains maximum hit points whenever magic is cast, proportional to spell level. [[Red Caesar]] casts Gust of Wind to clear the mist from the room, sacrificing his own maximum HP to protect the team. The party defeats all specters and the wraith. [[Domyx]] and [[Kerben]]'s basic weapons prove less effective against the wraith but still land hits. [[Ceril]]'s summoned beast spirit crab deals devastating critical hits.

After the fight, the Akasha quest resolves: Akasha is the pure substance of abjuration — the cancellation of magic and elements. It destroyed the old world's magic when Arcadia's oceans fell through the [[Hole in the Sky]], forming a layer at the ocean bottom. A theory emerges that [[Penumbra]] may be the crystallized form of liquid Akasha, and a new quest is added: create Penumbra from distilled Akasha.

The party explores additional rooms, finding a bedroom containing [[Deception by Lenth]] — a magical perfume once owned by [[Lenth the Rugged]] that grants advantage on deception checks for eight hours. [[Domyx]] takes it. Multiple arcane-locked doors are discovered, protected by the Guards and Wards spell, likely cast by the [[Nalfeshne]] to sequester itself. The spa/bath room can be used for short rests with curative properties (8 temp HP per member) if [[Kerben]] adds salt. Investigation reveals that after nobles fled during [[The Cataclysm]], common folk used the castle as refuge against rising waters for days or weeks.

### Chunk 0001

The party plans to ambush the [[Nalfeshne]] directly rather than continue a war of attrition. They decide to use [[Kerben]]'s magic key to cast Pass Wall through the ceiling from the floor above the Nalfeshne's throne room. The party takes a short rest in the spa to recover, gaining temporary hit points from the salt bath. They prepare buffs: [[Vokunar]] casts Calm Emotions to protect against the Nalfeshne's fear effect, [[Red Caesar]] casts Circle of Power to grant advantage on saves against magical effects, and [[Ceril]] summons a bestial spirit crab.

The party descends through the ceiling hole on ropes into a proper throne room. The room has stained glass windows overgrown with seaweed on the outside, stone pillars, benches, a red carpet ascending a dais, and wisps of remaining [[Akasha]] mist. A large chunk of [[Penumbra]] sits near the south wall. The Nalfeshne is caught completely off guard.

The Nalfeshne is described in detail: a boar-headed demon with extra tusks and horns growing outward in every direction like plant overgrowth, beady red eyes, a fur-covered muscular chest, and a wreath of smoky blue energy. It speaks Heavenly and taunts the party, saying it will "return this beautiful palace to Hell, where it belongs."

The party capitalizes on the surprise round. [[Kerben]]'s [[Zulu]] charges up the dais alongside the crab. [[Ceril]] fires Guiding Bolt to mark the target for favored enemy damage. [[Domyx]] engages with brutal strikes while enlarged to match the demon's size. [[Vokunar]] drops in with a superhero landing and casts Sacred Flame. The Nalfeshne teleports closer to [[Red Caesar]] using its blue fire reaction.

The battle intensifies. The Nalfeshne uses a legendary action to cast Melf's Acid Arrow, which [[Red Caesar]] partially Counterspells. [[Ceril]] casts Moonbeam, cooking the demon each round. The Nalfeshne unleashes its fear effect — a wave of psychic energy — but [[Vokunar]]'s Calm Emotions and [[Red Caesar]]'s Circle of Power completely insulate the party from both the fear and the psychic damage. The Nalfeshne focuses its attacks on [[Domyx]], who tanks the blows behind layers of temporary hit points. [[Red Caesar]] uses Shield to block a rending claw attack after the Nalfeshne teleports near him.

[[Vokunar]] places a Guardian of Faith to pressure the demon further. [[Kerben]] keeps his distance with ranged shots while directing [[Zulu]] and the crab. The crab continues its relentless assault, scoring critical hits. [[Ceril]]'s summoned bestial spirit crab delivers the killing blow — it charges up the stairs, clamps its pincers around the Nalfeshne's neck, and the demon dissipates in a puff of blue smoke.

After the fight, the party loots the throne room. The Nalfeshne's remains include a black periapt fashioned in Hell and set with sardonyx gems — a rare art object of otherworldly design. The throne itself holds a ruby and a sapphire set into the armrests. The party recovers the large [[Penumbra]] chunk, which has a blocky, fractal-like structure — multiple triangular fragments fused together.

With the Nalfeshne defeated, the castle's magical aura and abjurations weaken noticeably. The arcane locks that had been in place dissipate. The castle feels "surprisingly empty, kind of inert."

The party carries the Penumbra to the [[Gheister]]. Near [[Lyngbakr]], the stone weighs nearly nothing. They position the boat above the turtle's head and push the chunk overboard. [[Lyngbakr]] rumbles with a whale song, receiving the stone.

### Summary

The party executed a successful ambush of the [[Nalfeshne]] in its throne room within [[Castle Kaedon]]. By dropping through the ceiling from the floor above, they achieved complete surprise and overwhelmed the demon before it could react. [[Red Caesar]]'s Circle of Power proved decisive, soaking up magical damage and granting save advantages throughout the fight. [[Domyx]] absorbed the Nalfeshne's focused assault, while [[Ceril]]'s summoned crab — now a legendary party asset — delivered the killing blow. With the demon destroyed, the castle's fiendish protections collapsed. The party recovered a large [[Penumbra]] chunk from the throne room and delivered it to [[Lyngbakr]], continuing their mission to feed the turtle and raise the castle.

### Connections

- The Nalfeshne's destruction removes the primary fiendish threat occupying [[Castle Kaedon]], potentially opening the castle for deeper exploration.
- The crab summoned by [[Ceril]] has now been the killing blow in multiple major encounters (the [[Warg Lord]] and now the [[Nalfeshne]]), establishing it as a signature party asset.
- The Penumbra retrieved from the throne room is a new piece for [[Lyngbakr]], potentially enabling the castle to rise further when digested.
- The weakened abjurations and dissipated arcane locks suggest the castle's fiendish occupation was the primary force maintaining its current cursed state.
- The theory that [[Penumbra]] may be crystallized [[Akasha]] (from chunk 0000) reframes the Penumbra found in the Nalfeshne's lair — the demon may have been drawn to it as a power source.
- The black periapt with sardonyx gems is a rare Hell-forged art object, possibly tradeable or study-worthy.
