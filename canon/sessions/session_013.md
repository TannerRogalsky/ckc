# Session 013

## Events

- **Darvinblast aftermath:** The party reviewed the newly revealed [[Penumbra]] quarry near [[Darvinblast]]. Deep worlders began emerging into [[The Pit]] while other dwarves began moving into the underground, creating political unrest for [[Figma Brickfinger]] and [[Figma Brickfinger's Union]].
- **Ceril flashback:** A century ago, after [[Queen Caeradwyn]] died, [[Ceril]] fled west from [[Brimbalin]] with [[Vanzia Vinfei]] and other escaped captives. He alone saw thick east-to-west lines of light in the sky, which had been increasing before [[The Cataclysm]]. He rejected an overture from [[House Kiernodel]].
- **Domyx flashback:** During [[Domyx]]'s descent from the titan mountains, [[Ulrich Fjöler]] warned him that [[Clan Akathia]] had erased another blood relative who had already completed [[Touching the Sky]] and still existed somewhere in Stark.
- **Ship upgrades:** [[Obould]] ordered [[The Opal]] reinforced with a laminate deck and hull improvements so it could carry [[Kilosaurus]] and heavy [[Penumbra]].
- **Processing the Darvinblast Penumbra:** [[Vokunar]] used Detect Magic to find a kernel of pure sky at the center of the drained massive [[Penumbra]]. [[Figma Brickfinger]] and her dwarves shaved away the dead obsidian shell (volcanic black glass — sky energy turned mundane by misuse), leaving a smaller star-filled core. [[Kilosaurus]] — named by [[Kerben]] ("Kilo" meaning heavy, cow-like) — hauled it back to [[The Opal]].
- **Market upgrades:** [[Kerben]] commissioned the [[Hilltop Hunter]] from [[Sigrid Forgewelt]] and the [[Vivarian Zodex]] from [[Beryzoz Helmscar]]. [[Red Caesar]] traded for [[Beryzoz's Teeth]]. [[Ceril]] traded with [[Courteous Cam]] for dwarven seeds and a Call Lightning scroll.
- **New Penumbra lead:** [[Courteous Cam]] gave [[Ceril]] a map to a [[Penumbra]] chunk held in gnoll territory north of the dwarven lands.
- **Far Helm Clan site:** The party followed the [[Far Helm Clan]] map to an abandoned half-built coastal home. [[Red Caesar]] dated the abandonment to the rise of the deep worlder cult. [[Vokunar]] cast Detect Magic and See Invisibility — finding no magic but discovering [[Xander MacLenth]], a pirate ghost. [[Xander MacLenth]] revealed he and his crew died trying to seize the site from the dwarf family (likely ancestors of [[Gammix]] and [[Tammix]]). He mentioned his legendary father [[Lenth the Rugged]], a pirate with wives on every continent. [[Vokunar]] raised water with Create Water to release the ghost. They recovered a sapphire and some silver and copper coins.
- **Gnoll country:** The party entered [[Gnoll]] territory, moving into hills and difficult terrain with high weeds. [[Red Caesar]] cast Locate Creature targeting gnolls, sensing them to the north and preventing the ambush from springing. The party spotted three hidden gnoll groups. [[Kerben]] opened with [[Hilltop Hunter]] sniping and Hunter's Mark, using hide/unhide in the weeds for sneak attacks. [[Domyx]] pre-raged, jumped into terrain, and was swarmed — bitten and poisoned by gnoll fangs and hit by bone flails. [[Ceril]] cast Moonbeam (level 3) centered on [[Domyx]] and wild shaped into a constellation of stars. [[Vokunar]] used Fireball and Wall of Fire to clear clusters. Gnoll Pack Lords used Incite Rampage to direct their packs. The party gradually thinned the gnoll numbers. [[Vokunar]]'s Wall of Fire controlled movement effectively — gnolls had to leap through it to reach the party, and projectiles from beyond the wall burned up. The party eliminated all gnoll leaders first, leaving only stragglers. [[Domyx]] crit-punched a pack lord to death, hoisting the hyena and driving a fist into its jaw. [[Red Caesar]] split two weakened targets with Magic Missile in a double snipe. [[Kerben]] overkilled a remaining gnoll so severely its blood dispersed 40 feet. After the fight, [[Red Caesar]]'s Locate Creature confirmed no gnolls remained within 1000 feet in any direction. The party skipped a short rest so [[Kerben]] could retain Hunter's Mark concentration.
- **Penumbra hoard discovered:** [[Domyx]] spotted, tucked against the rocks, a large chunk of pure [[Penumbra]] already flooded with stars — untouched and full of energy, sitting atop a pile of gnomish gold bars (long, wafer-like coins unlike standard circular currency).
- **Wyvern guardians:** Two [[Wyvern]]s — horse-sized, armless dragon-like creatures with scorpion tails and concentrated poison — dove on the party from the peaks. [[Domyx]] attempted to grapple a flying wyvern using his trident in one-handed mode, catching a wing in the tines and pulling it to the ground. He was stung by the pinned wyvern's tail for 30 poison damage (reduced by his resistance but still dropped into the red). [[Ceril]] cast Protection from Poison on [[Domyx]], curing the poison and granting resistance for an hour. [[Ceril]] also summoned an aerial heron (Summon Beast, level 2) that attacked with advantage against the grappled wyvern, wild shaped into star form, and fired Luminous Arrows through the wall of fire. [[Red Caesar]] cast Shield of Faith and his abjuration ward absorbed 14 damage meant for [[Kerben]] when a wyvern chomped at him. He was stung by a wyvern but took only 18 damage thanks to massive poison resistance (though he gained poisoned status with no save, breaking his Locate Creature concentration). He attempted a level 5 Lightning Bolt through his magic wand but was limited to level 1; the wand still delivered significant damage. He learned that wyvern coloring indicates terrain camouflage, not resistances (wyverns resist only poison). [[Vokunar]] cast Aura of Vitality at 3rd level (30-foot radius) to heal the party, grew wings for flight, and used Toll the Dead to kill one wyvern. He healed [[Kerben]] for 10 HP. [[Kerben]] used his Assassin first-round advantage with Hunter's Mark for two attacks on the first turn — a powerful combo enabled by multiclassing into a class with Extra Attack. He later switched to melee, attacking with his whip for sneak attack damage and attempting Ensnaring Strike (level 2) to pin a wyvern (the wyvern succeeded on its save). He finished the last wyvern with a decisive ranged shot. [[Vokunar]] and [[Red Caesar]] detected the wyverns with Wisdom perception checks; [[Kerben]] knew how to extract poison from the wyvern so the meat could be eaten, and [[Ceril]] identified the sting as causing the poisoned status effect with concentrated poison.
- **Crew check-in:** [[Domyx]] played Titans Tarot (a card game he made into a crew initiation ritual) and checked in with the crew, making a charisma persuasion check to ease clashing personalities. [[Rochella Golf]] spoke about returning to Southport. [[Yalet Mora]] suggested recruiting a wind-aligned creature to help [[The Opal]] sail through dead winds — a quest was added. [[Relic Elnavi]] serves as the ship's janitor, using its blink ability to teleport trash.
- **Return to Lyngbakr Lagoon:** [[Relic Elnavi]] spotted a fire at [[Lyngbakr Lagoon]] and directed [[Domyx]] to look off the starboard side. The party found [[Alamar]] there with [[Tuna]], having developed shelters, water storage, torches, a repurposed irrigation chute for feeding stones to [[Lyngbakr]], and torches leading into the cave. [[Alamar]] survived an owlbear encounter and ate bear eggs for a week.
- **Alamar's warning:** [[Alamar]] reported that [[Broyish Empire]] airships had searched for [[Castle Kaedon]] but had not found it. He explained that he and [[Lyngbakr]] seem bound by a shared leash to the location; when they near its limit, a sequester-like sleepiness takes hold and both must return before losing their senses. He learned Windwall as a counter to airships. He sensed a powerful presence in the old throne room of [[Castle Kaedon]], as if the castle had a new king who had taken [[Penumbra]] beneath the waves. The party discussed *Mirage Arcana* as a potential camouflage spell for the castle.
- **Penumbra fed to Lyngbakr:** The party fed all collected [[Penumbra]] pieces to [[Lyngbakr]]. [[Alamar]] expects digestion to take weeks or a month, after which more of [[Castle Kaedon]] can rise for exploration.
- **Lagoon base established:** The party left [[Kilosaurus]], [[Relic Elnavi]], and three deckhands at [[Lyngbakr Lagoon]] to help [[Alamar]] build and defend a second base. [[Vokunar]] taught [[Alamar]] Continual Flame (paid for with a gold bar) and cast several continual flame torches for safe lighting. [[Obould]] took a longboat to transport the penumbra stones to the lagoon.
- **Consulting Sigil:** [[Vokunar]] contacted [[Sigil]] through his mantle — the colored gems turned to white diamonds and a brilliant sun rose for his eyes only. Only he could hear her answers. [[Red Caesar]] formulated a question about whether [[Alamar]] was concealing his true intentions; [[Sigil]] confirmed he is an ally but "has not told you every truth and he won't." [[Vokunar]] asked whether they would find allies along their planned route; [[Sigil]] said "our allies will appear." [[Domyx]] asked whether his ancestor who touched the sky was a possible enemy; [[Sigil]] confirmed that [[Domyx's Ancestor]] is the party's greatest enemy, perhaps their only great enemy. She advised heading first to the elves by the shortest wind-favored route and revealed that [[The Order of Seasons]] moved [[Penumbra]] beyond the jungles to a protected place. After the consultation, [[Sigil]]'s light fused back into [[Vokunar]]'s mantle.
- **Touching the Sky explained:** [[Domyx]] explained that among his people, touching the sky is an ambition contest — anyone who wants to be the best climber simply goes up as far as they can. There is no moral value to the act itself; it simply proves the climber is the best and their ambition is maximized. His ancestor was banished from home afterward and may have aimed for [[Arcadia]] itself. Since the sky is [[Aeris]]'s physical body, touching it means directly contacting the most powerful entity over Stark.
- **Blink Dog farewell:** [[Relic Elnavi]] sensed [[Ceril]]'s worry about venturing to the elven lands and offered that, as the closest thing to a high elf in the party, the elven world after [[The Cataclysm]] might not be worse than before. The blink dog blinked off, and [[Ceril]] chose to stay at the lagoon.
- **Route planning:** With [[Sigil]]'s advice, the party charted a course: four days north to the elves while winds favor that direction, then curving south past [[The Garden]], possibly visiting [[Cutlass Cray]] and [[The White Drake]] afterward. A trip to the east would take too long away from [[Castle Kaedon]].
- **Departure:** [[Obould]] prepared the longboat. The party said their goodbyes to the lagoon and set course for the coast of the central elven continent.

## Characters & Plot Summary

Session 013 closes the dwarven arc and pivots toward the elven arc. The party's work in [[Darvinblast]] freed the deep worlders but left [[The Pit]] politically unstable, so [[Figma Brickfinger]] helped with the [[Penumbra]] and encouraged the party to leave before they worsened tensions. The revealed [[Penumbra]] was not fully healthy: decades of use had turned its outer shell into mundane obsidian, making the remaining core much smaller but still valuable to [[Lyngbakr]].

The two flashbacks deepened the campaign's oldest mysteries. [[Ceril]]'s escape from the elven lands tied [[Queen Caeradwyn]]'s fall, elven slavery, and strange sky-lines to the period immediately before [[The Cataclysm]]. [[Domyx]]'s flashback revealed that [[Clan Akathia]] hid a prior sky-toucher from history. [[Sigil]] later confirmed the significance of that secret by naming [[Domyx's Ancestor]] the party's greatest enemy, suggesting that [[Touching the Sky]] and direct contact with [[Aeris]] may be tied to the broken sky.

The middle of the session was practical but important: [[The Opal]] was reinforced, [[Kerben]] and [[Red Caesar]] gained new magical equipment, [[Ceril]] gained seeds and a lightning spell, and the party recovered two major [[Penumbra]] sources. The [[Far Helm Clan]] site also showed how the deep worlder cult derailed ordinary dwarf lives. [[Xander MacLenth]]'s ghost added a ghostly remnant of pre-cataclysm piracy and introduced [[Lenth the Rugged]], a legendary pirate whose story the party chose not to pursue.

The gnoll and wyvern battles tested the party's coordination under heavy poison pressure. The gnoll fight showcased [[Kerben]]'s new Assassin multiclass combo (first-round advantage with Extra Attack), [[Domyx]]'s crit-punch finishing moves, and [[Vokunar]]'s Wall of Fire for area control. The wyvern encounter demanded aerial grappling, poison management, and abjuration shielding — [[Domyx]] pulled a flying wyvern to the ground with his trident, [[Red Caesar]]'s ward absorbed a wyvern bite meant for [[Kerben]], and [[Ceril]]'s heron summon and Protection from Poison kept the party functional. Both battles ended with the party choosing not to short rest, prioritizing maintaining spell concentrations and pressing forward.

The return to [[Lyngbakr Lagoon]] reframed [[Alamar]] from rescued curiosity into base-builder and local guardian. Feeding [[Lyngbakr]] advances the main quest, but it also increases the risk that [[Castle Kaedon]] will be discovered by [[Broyish Empire]] airships or [[The League of New Stark]]. The party responded by establishing the lagoon as a defended base with [[Relic Elnavi]], [[Kilosaurus]], and deckhands.

The session ends with the [[Sigil]] consultation providing strategic clarity. The goddess confirmed [[The Order of Seasons]] holds [[Penumbra]] deep in protected elven territory and named [[Domyx's Ancestor]] as the party's greatest enemy — perhaps their only great enemy. The consultation also revealed that [[Alamar]] is an ally but will never share every truth, and that allies will appear to support [[Lyngbakr Lagoon]]. [[Domyx]] explained the cultural context of [[Touching the Sky]] as a titan ambition contest with no inherent moral value, though his ancestor's feat meant physically touching [[Aeris]] herself — and he may have aimed for [[Arcadia]]. [[Relic Elnavi]] offered [[Ceril]] hope about the elven lands before blinking off. The party charted a four-day wind-favored route north to the elves, then curving south past [[The Garden]], and set sail.
