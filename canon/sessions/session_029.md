## Session 029

### Chunk 0000

The party returned to the rotunda and confirmed the first of four lamps was lit, with three more locations remaining. As the party prepared to enter another portal to the [[Magic Hat]], [[Kerben]] arrived — his alarm wards had been triggered. [[Ulrich Fjoller]] and [[Raxxy]] were approaching from [[The Opal]], bringing urgent news. Ulrich Fjoller reported that a [[Broyish Empire]] balloon had been spotted in the sky and scouts were near the island. Raxxy suggested the Empire might be casting a wide net, checking multiple locations rather than targeting [[The Carrot Cake]] specifically.

The party discussed whether the Empire knew their location. [[Red Caesar]] noted that [[Vizier Jade]] had previously sent [[The Tyrant]] to search for the park, and that The Tyrant never returned — suggesting the Empire may have suspicions about the area. Multiple layers of anti-scrying protection surround the party and [[The Opal]], but lingering at the park still risked discovery.

Kerben proposed that the adventurers continue exploring while he handled the boat. The party agreed, and Kerben departed with Ulrich Fjoller, Raxxy, and an unwilling [[Zulu]] to move [[The Opal]] away from the scouts.

The party entered the [[Magic Hat]] through a top hat portal. The area featured a black-and-white checkerboard tile floor with astroturf lawn, cracked in places where real plant life had grown through. Multi-colored lights (green, purple, orange) emanated from the north, with the orange glow of the carrot logo refracting off ponds. Two kiosks flanked the path: one staffed by a [[Magen]] endlessly shuffling a deck of cards, and another containing a broken fortune-teller machine with an automaton named [[Boston Golf]] — a porcelain-skinned figure with onyx eyes, inlaid with citrines and rubies. [[Red Caesar]] noted a striking resemblance between [[Boston Golf]] and [[Rochella Golf]], suggesting a family connection. The fortune-teller automaton was taken as treasure.

[[Red Caesar]] approached the magen dealer, who challenged him to a card game: draw from a 105-card deck (values 2–20, no 1s or 13s, with ten 20s) to get as close to 40 as possible without going over. Red Caesar spent time analyzing the deck's probabilities before play, and the entire party played against the dealer, making card counting easier. The party won all seven rounds through probabilistic skill and one fortunate draw. As a prize, Red Caesar received a magical scroll containing six divine spells, including Inflict Wounds, Warding Bond, Beacon of Hope, and Max Heal. The scroll was cleric-only magic, so Red Caesar gave it to [[Vokenar]].

Meanwhile, [[Ceril]] detected stirring in one of the water features. Creatures emerged: one undead and three living humanoids — all humans under magical protection. Ceril identified them as a vampire's ghouls or familiars, knowing they would need to be destroyed.

The battle escalated as a second water feature to the left bubbled up, producing three tiny [[Flame Skull]]s and a [[Gorgon Head]]. The gorgon head — a woman's face wreathed in snakes with no body — hovered and confused before attacking. [[Domyx]] used his [[Gith Shard Glaive]] to strike flame skulls across the battlefield. [[Red Caesar]] countered enemy magic, [[Vokenar]] protected Domyx from the gorgon's poison attacks and froze the flame skulls, and [[Ceril]] used chalice healing to keep Vokenar alive.

The party eliminated the vampiric familiars and flame skulls. The gorgon head remained the final threat, trading blows with Domyx while Red Caesar pressed it with magic. When the gorgon was finally killed, its head burst in a wave of black-and-white petrifying energy that began turning Domyx and Vokenar to stone. Combat was ongoing at the end of the chunk.

### Chunk 0001

The gorgon head petrification effect resolved: [[Domyx]] faked being a statue briefly for dramatic effect, then burst free. [[Vokenar]] used Lesser Restoration on himself to clear the petrification. The party took a short rest at the [[Magic Hat]] hotel.

The party explored the hotel interior — three levels with approximately 30 rooms connected by a spiral staircase. Amenities included a chocolate fountain, soft serve ice cream station, shrimp cocktail, and mini bar. [[Ceril]] found a purple worm hide rug with amethysts and carrot-infused tobacco (a hybrid plant). [[Vokenar]] used Arcane Eye to scout the hotel and found [[Ramadi's Capers]] issue 4 — a collectible comic about a dashing goblin rogue — unfurled on a bed. A collector exists who trades for the complete set.

The party long-rested at the hotel. A [[Magen]] maitre d' read their minds and delivered personalized food to each room. By morning, [[Kerben]] sent a message via animal messenger warning that [[Broyish Empire]] scouts may be specifically investigating this location, not just casting a wide net. He urged the party to hurry.

The party performed a protective feast (Heroes' Feast). The magen maitre d' hurried them out with a 10:30 checkout time, casting Guards and Wards and Arcane Lock on the hotel. [[Red Caesar]] flipped the Magic Hat lamp lighter switch — the magen powered down and back up, confirming it did not require electricity. This lit the second of four lamps needed for [[Jack Harvey]]'s inner sanctum.

The party entered [[King of the Hole]] through a portal, emerging on a small island surrounded by ocean with a grassy hill. A [[Magen]] announcer — dressed differently from other magens, holding a carrot that amplified his voice — welcomed them with researched introductions for each party member. He explained the wave-based combat system: five beacon lights (carrot-shaped) are positioned around the island, and three must be pressed simultaneously to start each wave. Four waves are required, with an optional fifth bonus wave granting access to Jack's lamplighter. The enemies are presented not by [[Jack Harvey]] but by his benefactor [[Faraday]], who wanted the park to remain entertaining even after the apocalypse.

The party spread out to the beacons and pressed the first combination. The first wave spawned two [[Phase Cat]]s, a [[Gorgon Head]], a [[Flame Skull]], and mummies. The enemies forced the party to fight across the battlefield instead of holding a single formation. Domyx and Vokenar pressured the airborne threats while Red Caesar and Ceril handled enemies at range. Combat was ongoing at the end of the chunk.

### Chunk 0002

The party finished the first wave of [[King of the Hole]], defeating the remaining [[Mummy]]s and the [[Gorgon Head]]. [[Domyx]] used his [[Gith Shard Glaive]] to cleave through enemies, while [[Red Caesar]] used his final Potion of Longstrider combined with [[Vokenar]]'s Water Walk to sprint across the ocean surface to his beacon.

The party triggered the second wave. This wave spawned [[Hooksy the Clown Automaton]]s, [[Death Spark]]s, a [[Shambling Mound]], and a [[Lightning Elemental]]. [[Vokenar]] opened with holy radiance against the clustered enemies. [[Red Caesar]] accelerated himself and sprinted up walls and across water to reach the far beacons. [[Ceril]] transformed into a dragon in constellation form, attacking from above. [[Domyx]] used his cloud jaunt to swap places with Vokenar, placing himself directly in the enemy cluster.

Red Caesar used Blight against the [[Shambling Mound]], withering the plant creature. The [[Lightning Elemental]]'s arcs healed the shambling mound rather than damaging it, creating a dangerous synergy. The party methodically eliminated the death sparks and Hooksy variants before Domyx destroyed the lightning elemental with his glaive, suffering its electrical death throes.

Between waves, [[Vokenar]] healed the party, preparing them for the next encounter. The party positioned at beacons for the third wave, which spawned more Hooksy variants and another shambling mound. The chunk ended as the third wave appeared.

### Chunk 0003

The third wave of [[King of the Hole]] spawned [[Hooksy the Clown Automaton]]s, a [[Shambling Mound]], a [[Cloaker]], and a [[Phase Cat]]. The party engaged across the island terrain. [[Domyx]] was wrapped and lifted by the cloaker but fought his way free. [[Red Caesar]] used supernatural speed to sprint between targets, striking both the cloaker and a Hooksy variant. [[Vokenar]] grew wings and flew into position.

A key discovery emerged: when a Hooksy variant died from [[Vokenar]]'s radiant fireball, it burst open bloodily and released two smaller Hooksy variants from within. The smaller versions resembled crab-sized forms covered in sticky placental fluid. [[Domyx]] destroyed one before it could split further, and [[Ceril]] melted the second with acid, sterilizing any remaining inner Hooksy variants. Tiny surviving forms scuttled into the ocean, no longer an immediate threat.

With the third wave defeated, confetti burst into the air and the fight announcer declared the victory. The party took a short rest, aided by [[Vokenar]]'s Divine Intervention. They discussed button combinations for the next wave. [[Domyx]] explored a 40-foot lighthouse on the island and looted a giant carrot-shaped glass lightbulb. As the party prepared to press the next beacon combination, dramatic music played and [[Kerben]] appeared from one of the portals, signaling the next encounter. The session ended on this cliffhanger.

### Summary

The party entered the [[Magic Hat]] zone of [[The Carrot Cake]], the second of four areas to explore. [[Kerben]] alerted them to [[Broyish Empire]] scouts nearby before departing with [[Ulrich Fjoller]] and [[Raxxy]] to handle the ship. Inside the Magic Hat, the party explored a surreal checkerboard-and-astroturf landscape with magical kiosks. [[Red Caesar]] analyzed and won a probability-based card game against a [[Magen]] dealer, earning a scroll of six powerful divine spells for [[Vokenar]]. The party was ambushed by enemies from the area's water features: vampiric familiars (living humans under vampire protection), [[Flame Skull]]s, and a [[Gorgon Head]]. [[Domyx]]'s [[Gith Shard Glaive]] proved devastating with its 60-foot cleave range, striking distant flame skulls. [[Vokenar]] was nearly killed by fireballs but was saved by [[Ceril]]'s chalice healing. The gorgon head was defeated but burst with petrifying energy; Domyx and Vokenar both survived the effect.

The party explored the Magic Hat hotel interior, finding treasures including a purple worm hide rug with amethysts and [[Ramadi's Capers]] issue 4. After a long rest, Kerben warned that the Empire may be specifically targeting their location. The party flipped the Magic Hat lamp switch (second of four lamps lit) and entered [[King of the Hole]] — an island arena with a wave-based combat system set up by [[Faraday]], [[Jack Harvey]]'s benefactor. Five beacons must be activated three at a time to trigger waves of enemies. The party fought through three waves: phase cats and mummies, Hooksy variants with a shambling mound and lightning elemental, and Hooksy variants with a cloaker and phase cat. During the third wave, the party discovered that Hooksy variants can burst open on death to release smaller forms from within — a disturbing mitotic reproduction. The session ended with Kerben appearing from a portal, signaling the next encounter.
